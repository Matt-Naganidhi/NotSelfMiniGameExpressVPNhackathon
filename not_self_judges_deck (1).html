JUDGING CRITERION ① — RELEVANCE TO CHALLENGE Theme: Secure by Design · Privacy-First Digital Safety for Young Users

Every current age-gate harms the users it protects

🪪
ID-based gates
Collect passport scans & selfies from children. Massive PII honeypots. Excludes unbanked & undocumented minors. Breaks the privacy-first mandate of this hackathon.
✗ Antithetical to Secure-by-Design
🤖
CAPTCHA gates
Solved by 7-year-olds in seconds. Trivially defeated by AI vision models in 2024. Discriminates users with motor or vision disabilities. Tests puzzle ability, not maturity.
✗ Measures nothing about safety
😶
Emotion-face gates
Meta-analysis of 148 studies shows autism involves non-selective facial emotion recognition impairment — not a maturity signal. Spoofed by AI face generators.
✗ Discriminates neurodivergent users
🎯
Our submission directly addresses the stated problem
The hackathon asks for safety mechanisms that do not depend on centralized tracking, invasive monitoring, or excessive data collection. That is exactly what we build.
ASD facial recognition meta-analysis (148 studies) · ScienceDirect 2021: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2021.11.038

Parts Mirror — identify your inner family, prove your maturity

1
Scenario prompt
"Your pull request was rejected with harsh feedback." An emotionally activating context is displayed.
2
Tap which parts activate
Emoji-labeled part chips (😤 Angry, 😔 Ashamed, 😰 Scared, 😤 Defensive…). Player taps all that are present.
3
Self or part speaking?
Binary card pick: ✨ "My Self is present and observing" vs "I AM the emotion right now." This is the maturity signal.
4
Curiosity slider
Drag toward "Curious about this part" or away ("Hostile / avoiding"). Curiosity = Self-led relationship quality.
Score: Parts count (awareness) + differentiation (Self vs blended) + curiosity rating → parts_band

JUDGING CRITERION ⑤ — IMPACT & USEFULNESS

Two impact vectors: young user safety + open source security

👶 Young user safety
A child who cannot yet reflect on their anger as a part separate from their Self receives guided access — not exclusion. The game itself develops the awareness it measures. Playing is therapeutic.
Lv 1–5 · Blended → Guided/supervised access only
Lv 6–15 · Aware → Standard content access
Lv 16–20 · Self-led → Community contributor role
Access is non-punitive: Lower bands receive scaffolded environments. The game incentivises self-awareness growth — a positive feedback loop for child development.
🔐 Open source community security
Carnegie Mellon CERT: most insider incidents begin as unacknowledged emotional states — anger, shame, grievance — not malice. A maintainer's activated Firefighter part (triggered by criticism) quietly introduces regressions. This is the real risk vector, not external hackers.
IFS lens on insider threat
Burdened Exile (shame) + Firefighter (impulsive commit) = insider risk — regardless of technical access level. Self-led contributors communicate; blended contributors act out.
Result: Psychologically safe, Self-led maintainer culture → fewer grudge commits, earlier conflict surfacing, more resilient open source communities.
Carnegie Mellon CERT Insider Threat Center · Anti, Univ. Vaasa 2025: https://techxplore.com/news/2025-11-stress-toxic-workplace-culture-insider.html · Ponemon 2023: 55% of insider threats are emotional/negligent

RESEARCH FOUNDATION — ALL CITATIONS

7 peer-reviewed sources — every claim is backed

IFS Evidence Base (2015 NREPP · 2025 scoping review)
IFS designated evidence-based by SAMHSA NREPP. 2025 Clinical Psychologist scoping review confirms IFS as "promising therapeutic approach" with breadth of clinical applications. Validates framework grounding all 5 games.
https://ifs-institute.com/resources/research · https://doi.org/10.1080/13284207.2025.2533127
ASD Facial Emotion Recognition — Meta-analysis (2021)
148-study meta-analysis: autism involves non-selective FER impairment across ALL basic emotions — not maturity-related. Mandates our autism-inclusive design (no face tasks). Frontiers in Child & Adolescent Psychiatry 2024 replicates finding.
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2021.11.038 · https://doi.org/10.3389/frcha.2024.1520854
SAFE Model — Psychological Maturity Scale (PMC 2024)
Gencer & Kaya: validated 4-factor model of psychological maturity — Self-Awareness, Autonomy, Flexibility, Ego Resilience. Self-Awareness is the primary factor. Grounds our parts_band scoring dimensions.
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/ (search: SAFE psychological maturity scale Gencer 2024)
REThink RCT — In-game emotion reappraisal transfers to real life (2022)
David et al. Frontiers in Psychiatry: RCT showing cognitive restructuring performed in a game transfers measurably to real-world emotional regulation. Directly validates Parts Dialogue game design.
https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyt.2022.773071
Zoo U — Game-based SEL assessment (2018)
DeRosier & Thomas J. Applied Developmental Psychology: in-game social scenario scores are valid vs. teacher-rated social-emotional skills across grade bands. Establishes criterion validity of game-based social appraisal measurement.
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.appdev.2018.01.002
Insider Threat Research (Carnegie Mellon CERT · Anti 2025)
CERT: insider incidents begin as frustration → grievance → rationalization. Vaasa 2025 (Anti): stress & toxic culture drive insider deviance, not malice. Ponemon 2023: 55% of insider threats are emotional/negligent. Grounds open-source security reframe.
https://techxplore.com/news/2025-11-stress-toxic-workplace-culture-insider.html · https://arxiv.org/html/2407.05943v1
